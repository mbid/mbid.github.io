Here's a possibly more conceptual prove for why uniform fibrant replacement preserves property \(P\) in Thierry's notes: Unfolding the small object argument, it suffices to check that the property is preserved under gluing all not already defined fillers to open boxes \(f : \sqcap^n_{i, e} \rightarrow Q\) onto a cubical set \(Q\). Here, \(\sqcap^n_{i, e}\) is the \(n\)-dimensional box open in dimension i and direction \(e \in \{0, 1\}\).

For 2-cubes in \(Q\) we use the hypothesis \(P(Q)\). We will only get additional 2-simplices in dimensions \(n \le 3\). 2-boxes arising from fillings of 2- and 1-open boxes will never be symmetric, so there is nothing to show in these cases.

Let \(n = 3\) and fix the variable set \(\{i, j, k\}\); \(f\) is an open in dimension \(i\) and direction \(e\). The 2-simplices arising from fillings of 3-open boxes are of the form \[ x = \operatorname{fill}^n_{i, e}(f) (i = e) \] Denote by \(j \leftrightarrow k : \{i, j, k\} \rightarrow \{i, k , j\}\) the obvious exchange of dimensions. Then \[ \begin{aligned} & x (j \leftrightarrow k) = x \\ \iff & f (j \leftrightarrow k) = f \\ \iff & \operatorname{fill}^n_{i, e}(f) (j \leftrightarrow k) = \operatorname{fill}^n_{i, e}(f) \end{aligned} \]

by uniformity. The first \(\Rightarrow\) and the second \(\Leftarrow\) are because we glued the fillers to \(Q\) /freely/. Thus, the filler provides a homotopy \[ x \sim \operatorname{fill}^n_{i, e}(f) (i = 1 - e) =: y, \] and we already have a chain of homotopies \(y \sim \dots \sim \text{pt}\) that is invariant under \(j \leftrightarrow k\) because \(y \in Q\).

Note that the proof will also work for general (possibly non-uniform) fibrations; there the 2-simplices arising from fillers will simply never be symmetric. I don't think this is a problem though because if I interpret the thread above correctly, uniformly fibrant \(\iff\) fibrant for cartesian cubical sets.
